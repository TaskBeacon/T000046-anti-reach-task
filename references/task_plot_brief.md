# Task Plot Brief

## Task

- Title: Anti-Reach Task
- Construct: inhibitory control / spatial transformation / reference-frame control
- Paradigm: pro/anti reach-direction proxy responses under standard and generalized eye/hand arrangements.

## Rows

- Standard pro: eye and hand anchors on same side; respond same direction as cue.
- Standard anti: eye and hand anchors on same side; respond opposite cue.
- Generalized pro: eye and hand anchors on opposite sides; respond same direction as cue.
- Generalized anti: eye and hand anchors on opposite sides; respond opposite cue.

## Trial Timeline

1. Fixation: 500-1000 ms. Show eye fixation and hand anchor, no response.
2. Context cue: 200 ms. Show green pro frame or blue anti frame.
3. Memory hold: 500-1500 ms. Hold fixation after frame removal.
4. Go cue: 170 ms. Flash white spatial cue.
5. Reach response: 1000 ms. Press F for left or J for right as reach proxy.
6. Feedback: 300 ms. Show correct goal marker and outcome label.
7. ITI: 600 ms neutral fixation.

## Notes

- Conditions span standard/generalized arrangement, pro/anti rule, and cue side left/right.
- Keyboard responses are a portable proxy for reach direction relative to the hand anchor.
